## 🎯 Overall Reactions — Whole-Experience Reflection

After completing all phases, take 15–20 minutes to reflect on the experience as a whole.

### 1. Elevator pitch test

In your own words, what does VolunteerCal do?

> _(This tests whether the value prop is clear from using the product. Don't look at the landing page — answer from memory.)_



### 2. Audience fit

Who is this clearly built for? Who would struggle with it?



### 3. Comparison

If you've used Planning Center, SignUpGenius, Breeze, Rock RMS, or just a spreadsheet for volunteer scheduling — how does VolunteerCal compare? What does it do better? What does it do worse?



### 4. The "scariest" feature

Which feature felt the most powerful but also the most risky to use? (e.g., "Auto-draft is impressive but I'd want a 'preview without committing' option before I'd trust it.")



### 5. The "missing" feature

What's the one thing that — if added — would push this from "nice to have" to "must buy" for a real church?



### 6. The "delete" feature

Is there anything you'd cut entirely because it adds confusion without commensurate value?



### 7. Mobile vs. desktop

Which experience felt more polished? Where did mobile fall short?



### 8. Pricing reaction

Looking at the pricing tiers (Free $0, Starter $29, Growth $69, Pro $119) — does it feel fair? Too expensive? Too cheap? Are the tier breakpoints in the right places?



### 9. Trust signals

Did anything during the test make you doubt the platform's reliability, security, or staying power?



### 10. Final word

Anything else you want Jason to know? One-liners welcome. Stream of consciousness welcome. Ranting welcome.
