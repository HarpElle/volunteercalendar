# B1 — `b1-apple-wallet-pass`

## Adding your family's check-in pass to Apple Wallet

The check-in pass is a quick way to identify your family at the kiosk — no typing a phone number or carrying a printed card. This guide gets it onto your iPhone and explains the one bit of "how does it know?" magic.

**Before you start:** your church needs to have you registered as a guardian, and you'll need an iPhone. (An Android version is on the way but isn't available yet.)

---

### What the pass is

It's a permanent ID for your household. At the kiosk, you hold it up to scan instead of entering the last four digits of your phone or scanning a printed QR card.

---

### Adding it

1. Open the guardian portal link your church sent you
2. Tap **Add to Apple Wallet**
3. Tap **Add** on the screen iOS shows you

[SCREENSHOT: b1-guardian-portal-add-button]

[SCREENSHOT: b1-ios-wallet-add-sheet]

That's it — the pass now lives in your Wallet.

---

### The location-aware part

When your iPhone notices you're within about 100 meters of the church, the pass surfaces on your lock screen with "Check in at" and your campus name. One tap opens it in Wallet, ready to scan. You don't have to go hunting for it.

[SCREENSHOT: b1-pass-on-lock-screen-near-church]

---

### What's on the pass

- Your family name
- Your children's first names
- A household barcode the kiosk reads
- Your church's logo, if they've uploaded one

---

### A note on the security code

The 4-character pickup code is **not** on the pass. That code changes with every check-in and is sent to you separately by text. The two do different jobs: the pass identifies your household, and the code authorizes releasing your child.

---

### Keeping it up to date

For now, the pass is a snapshot from when you added it. If your family name changes or a new child joins, open the guardian portal again and tap **Add to Apple Wallet** to refresh it. (Automatic updates are a planned improvement.)
