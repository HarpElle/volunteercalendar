# B5 — `b5-attendance-taking`

## Marking who's actually in your room

### Why this matters

A child can be checked in at the kiosk but not physically in your room — they wandered off with a sibling, they're in the bathroom, they slipped out. In an emergency, the evacuation marshal or EMT needs to know that, so they don't waste time searching your room for a child who isn't there. Marking attendance is how that information reaches them.

---

### The two pills

Each child's row on the teacher dashboard has two pills:

- **Present** — sage when active
- **Not in room** — amber when active

[SCREENSHOT: b5-attendance-pills-unmarked]

Tap a pill to set it. Tap the active pill again to clear it back to unmarked. The two are independent, but only one can be active at a time — the system enforces that for you.

[SCREENSHOT: b5-attendance-pills-present]

[SCREENSHOT: b5-attendance-pills-not-in-room]

If two teachers in the same room mark the same child differently within about 30 seconds, the most recent mark wins.

---

### When to mark each one

- **Present** — once you've physically counted the child in your room
- **Not in room** — when a child was checked in at the kiosk but hasn't actually appeared in your space
- **Clear it** — when a child you'd marked absent shows up

---

### Where it shows up

Your marks flow straight to the emergency roster:

- **Not in room** becomes a bold amber **REPORTED ABSENT FROM ROOM** badge
- **Present** becomes a subtle sage **Confirmed present** badge
- Unmarked children show no badge
