# B2 — `b2-pickup-ready-ping`

## Letting the room know you're here for pickup

When the children's area is secured and parents can't walk back to the classroom, this lets a parent signal the room from the lobby — and lets the teacher respond. This guide has a section for each side.

---

## For parents

### Why this exists

If the children's-ministry area is behind a locked door, you can't just walk to the classroom to collect your child. Instead of finding a staff member to radio the room, you send the signal yourself from the kiosk.

### What to do

1. Walk up to the kiosk and find your household (any lookup method works)
2. Tap **I'm here for pickup** — the sage-green button on the child-selection screen, above **Back** and **Next**
3. Wait in the lobby with your pickup code ready

[SCREENSHOT: a2-kiosk-pickup-ready-button-visible]

[SCREENSHOT: b2-kiosk-pickup-ready-success]

### One thing to know

This is just a heads-up to the room — it doesn't release your child on its own. At the kiosk, the volunteer still matches your security code to the code on your child's name tag before handing them over. Have the code ready.

---

## For teachers

### Seeing the signal

Your teacher dashboard refreshes every 30 seconds. When a parent sends the signal, that child's row changes to a coral background with a coral ring and a header reading **Parent here for pickup**. A sage-green **On my way** button appears at the start of the row's buttons.

[SCREENSHOT: a3-teacher-view-pickup-ready-state]

### Responding

Tap **On my way**. The row flips to a sage **Acknowledged** state, which tells the other teachers — and any admin watching the room view — that someone's handling it. Then walk the child to the lobby and go through the normal checkout.

[SCREENSHOT: a3-teacher-view-acknowledged-state]

---

### If something doesn't go as expected

**(Parent) "I sent it but no one came."** The teacher may not have acknowledged yet — the row updates within 30 seconds. If there's still no response after a few minutes, find a staff member.

**(Teacher) "Another teacher and I both tapped On my way."** That's fine. The latest tap wins, and both phones show the same acknowledged state on the next refresh.
