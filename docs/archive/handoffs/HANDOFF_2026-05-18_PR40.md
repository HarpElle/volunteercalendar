## 1. What shipped

### Polish #1 — Trainee fill-rate live recompute

**Before:** the top stats bar on `/dashboard/schedules` baked in `filled_slots` / `fill_rate` / `unfilled_slots` at generation/view time. When an admin flipped a chip to Trainee from the matrix menu, the per-role `1× Role needed` chip correctly reappeared (PR #27 already did that), but the stats bar still read e.g. `3/3 · 100% · 0 unfilled` — a visible numeric inconsistency that any UX reviewer would catch immediately.

**After:** new `liveStats` useMemo in `schedules/page.tsx` derives `filled` / `fill_rate` / `unfilled_slots` / `unique_volunteers` / `by_status` from `activeAssignments + activeStats.total_slots` on every render. Trainees are excluded from `filled` per the matrix convention — they shadow the slot, they don't fill it. `total_slots` stays sourced from the once-computed `activeStats` (it's about the services × date-range expansion, not affected by per-assignment mutations). `fairness_score` also stays stable (gen-time distribution).

**Net effect:** any mutation (Trainee toggle, Reassign, Unassign) now reflows the stats bar naturally without a page reload.

### Polish #2 — Availability banner auto-clear when due_date < today

**Before:** `/dashboard/my-schedule` rendered every unread `availability_request` notification as a banner, regardless of `metadata.due_date`. Volunteers saw stale "Submit Availability by Mar 5" prompts after Mar 5 had come and gone.

**After:** the render-time filter skips banners whose `due_date < today`. The notification itself stays in `user_notifications` for inbox history (the admin's record of who saw it is preserved); the banner just doesn't render past the deadline.
